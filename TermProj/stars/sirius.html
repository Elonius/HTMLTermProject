Sirius

  • The force of gravity on Sirius B is 350,000 stronger than on Earth
    • Meaning 3 grams of matter (roughly a sugar cube) would weigh 2,200 pounds!
  • Sirius is the brightest star in the night sky
    • And the nearest that can be seen without the aid of a telescope.
  • The derivation of the name Sirius
    • Is from the ancient Greek word for glowing, the star was also known to ancient Egyptians 4,000 years ago.
  • Sirius is a White Dwarf
    • White dwarfs are the core remains of stars that have exhausted their fuel and shed their outer layers.
  • Sirius is a binary star system consisting of two white stars
    • Orbiting each other with a separation of about 20 AU (roughly the distance between the Sun and Uranus) and a period of 50.1 years.
