Polaris

  • Polaris is a three star system
    • Comprising of the supergiant Polaris A and two smaller companions.
  • Polaris is the only star in the night sky that appears to remain stationary.
    • This is because it is currently positioned above the northern axis of Earth's rotation.
  • As the star always points north
    • It has been an aid to navigators for centuries, although it is only visible in the northern hemisphere.
  • Polaris has been aligned above the North Pole for around 2,000 years
    • But after 2100 Earth's northern axis will begin to move away from the star.
  • Polaris is approximately 430 light years from Earth
    • And is the brightest star in the constellation of Ursa Minor.
